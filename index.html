→ Questions, briefly

FAQ.

Is this another calorie tracker?
No. Trackers make you log what you ate. Mise en Planner goes the other direction — it tells you what to eat, portioned to hit your daily macros. You don't weigh food, you don't log anything, you don't argue with a database about whether that was a medium or large apple.
How are my macros calculated?
Standard Mifflin-St Jeor BMR from your height, weight, age, and sex, multiplied by an activity factor, then adjusted for your goal (lose / maintain / gain) and pace. Protein is scaled to your bodyweight. Nothing exotic.
What about allergies and diets?
During onboarding you mark anything off the table — vegetarian, vegan, pescatarian, gluten-free, nut allergy, dairy, soy, shellfish, wheat, and so on. Your plan respects all of it.
Can I swap meals I don't like?
Tap any meal, hit Swap, pick from a short list of alternatives. We only offer swaps that keep your day's macros in range.
Do I need to cook?
You tell us your patience level during onboarding — under 10 minutes, around 20, or "I actually enjoy this." Your plan is scoped accordingly.
When does it launch?
Summer 2026, iPhone first. Beta testers get it free for the duration. Join the waitlist below.
Is this medical advice?
No. Mise en Planner is a meal planning tool for generally healthy adults. If you have specific dietary needs, a medical condition, are pregnant, or are under 18, please talk to a registered dietitian or your doctor.
